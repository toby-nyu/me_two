My Teaching

Overview

I have over 15 years of experience teaching programming and computer science to a diverse range of students, from young children to adult learners. My teaching philosophy focuses on practical skills, real-world applications, and fostering a supportive learning environment.

In addition to my teaching roles, I have also contributed to curriculum development and educational resources, ensuring that my students receive up-to-date and relevant instruction in the ever-evolving field of technology.

NYU London

At NYU London, I teach the course Introduction to Web Design and Computer Principles, which is part of the NYU Computer Science curriculum. The course introduces students to the fundamentals of web development and computing principles, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Linux file systems.

Students learn client-side scripting, control structures, loops, functions, and the Document Object Model (DOM).

The course prepares students to build interactive, secure, and powerful web applications, laying the foundation for careers in web development, software engineering, and digital design.

NYU London is the UK campus of New York University, offering American-style education in the heart of London. It provides a unique international experience for students studying abroad.

University of London via Coursera

I teach Object-Oriented Programming in C++ for the University of London via Coursera, in conjunction with Goldsmiths.

This online program supports students worldwide in developing strong programming skills and understanding core computer apprenticeships.

My role involves assessing student work and providing constructive feedback to help learners build foundational coding skills and technical confidence.

Learn more about the program at University of London on Coursera.

Code Club UK

I run Code Clubs for younger learners, helping children aged 9–13 learn to code through fun and engaging projects. Code Club UK is a nationwide initiative that promotes digital literacy and creativity. Learn more at Code Club UK.

City, University of London

Since 2022, I’ve taught programming modules at City, University of London, focusing on JavaScript and web technologies. I use live coding, real-world examples, and interactive exercises to engage students. City is a leading London university known for its computing, engineering, and business programs.

Birkbeck, University of London

I taught at Birkbeck from 2014 until July 2025, specialising in undergraduate programming courses including web development, Java, and PHP. Birkbeck is part of the University of London and is known for its evening education model, supporting adult learners and working professionals.

USP College

At USP College in Essex, I deliver BTEC, T-Level, and A-Level curriculum in computing and IT. I also liaise with local employers to create career opportunities for students. USP College is committed to practical learning and preparing students for the tech industry.
